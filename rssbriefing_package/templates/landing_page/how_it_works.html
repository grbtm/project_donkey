    So, how does this actually work? 🤔


    1. Humans collect and curate trending topics

    We collect the trending headlines of the past 24 hours from a wide range of news sources. We then handpick a small number of topics that become the set of current trending news. We focus on the most important global headlines.

    2. AI selects the most relevant posts of the past 24h from your feeds

    Based on the list of a user's source feeds, a machine learning model picks the posts that are most similar to our set of trending news.

    3. AI summarizes the chosen articles

    Then we collect the full articles from the selected posts and use another machine learning model to summarize the articles. In the end, you get a summary of each trending topic based on your list of news sources.

    4. rssBriefing delivers the briefing to each user

    Now that the custom briefing has been generated, you can log in to your rssBriefing account and view your personal briefing.



    Technologies we use:

    gensim
    We trained a document similarity model based on gensim's implementation of Doc2Vec and we use gensim's built-in summarization model.
    Flask
    The app is written using the Python micro web framework Flask.
    feedparser
    We use feedparser to process RSS and Atom feeds.
    newspaper3k
    The newspaper3k library allows us to parse the actual news articles.
    AWS Elastic Beanstalk
    For hosting.
